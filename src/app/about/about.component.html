Who We Are

The LA Ocean Coalition is a volunteer organization dedicated to the preservation of our planet’s oceans. We believe the journey toward protecting our oceans begins at home. Through our work promoting sustainability on campus at the University of California, Los Angeles, we hope to make a change for the positive in our community, and act as a catalyst for sustainability across the globe.

We Need Your Help

We can't do it alone. Here are three ways you can support our mission to preserve our planet's oceans.

I. Sign Our Petition

We are currently focusing our efforts to reduce UCLA’s oceanic footprint by lessening the plastic waste that our campus produces. We are working to eliminate the use of polystyrene, a substance that makes up products such as plastic straws, plastic cups, and styrofoam. Polystyrene never fully decomposes in the ocean, and breaks down into microscopic bits that pollute our oceans and the bodies of the animals who live in them. (If you eat fish or other seafoods, these bits of plastic can end up in your body as well. Yuck!)

Read more about the harmful effects of polystyrene here, and sign our polystyrene petition.

II. Make a Contribution

You may have heard money makes the world go round. We’re here to tell you, it also keeps our oceans clean. Any funds contributed to our gofundme are put toward preserving our beautiful oceans.

III. Get Involved

Join us as we try to fight plastics pollution with boots on the ground. Volunteer with us and come to our first of many beach cleanups hosted on October 20th in Santa Monica. If you crave to do more, become a team ambassador and apply for one of our open positions within the LA Ocean Coalition admin.
